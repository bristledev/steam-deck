#  External Resources (Keep Learning!)

You’ve made it through the entire series! You now have a solid foundation for using, managing, and optimizing your Steam Deck like a pro. But the world of SteamOS and Linux is vast, and there’s always more to discover.

Here are the best places to continue your journey:

## The "Big Three" Communities
If you have a specific question or want to see what other people are doing with their Decks, these are the best places to go:
- **[r/SteamDeck (Reddit)](https://www.reddit.com/r/SteamDeck/)**: The largest collection of Steam Deck owners in the world. Great for news, show-off posts, and general troubleshooting.
- **[Steam Deck Discord](https://discord.gg/steamdeck)**: A very active real-time community where you can get help with specific technical issues.
- **[Steam Deck HQ](https://steamdeckhq.com/)**: A website dedicated to testing games and providing "best settings" for performance.

## Technical Guides & Documentation
Want to go deeper into the tools we’ve discussed? These are the official sources:
- **[Tailscale Documentation](https://tailscale.com/kb/1011/install-steamos/)**: Excellent, beginner-friendly guides for everything related to networking.
- **[Distrobox GitHub](https://github.com/89luca89/distrobox)**: The best place to find advanced tips for running other Linux distributions.
- **[NixOS Wiki](https://nixos.wiki/)**: If you chose the "immutable powerhouse" path, this is your bible. It’s dense, but worth it.
- **[EmuDeck Wiki](https://emudeck.github.io/)**: The definitive source for everything related to emulation on the Steam Deck.

## YouTube Channels
Sometimes it’s easier to see things in action. These creators are dedicated to the Steam Deck:
- **[The Phawx](https://www.youtube.com/@ThePhawx)**: Deep dives into technical performance and hardware.
- **[Retro Game Corps](https://www.youtube.com/@RetroGameCorps)**: The gold standard for emulation guides and "cozy" Steam Deck content.
- **[GamingOnLinux](https://www.youtube.com/@GamingOnLinux)**: Great for keeping up with the latest SteamOS updates and Linux gaming news.

## Final Advice: Be Fearless!
The single best resource is your own curiosity. Because of tools like **[Distrobox](https://github.com/89luca89/distrobox)** and **[Podman](https://podman.io/)**, you can experiment without fear. 

Don't be afraid to break things (in a container!), try new shells, or customize the UI until it’s perfect. That’s the beauty of the Steam Deck—it’s not just a console; it’s *your* Linux PC.

**Happy Gaming!**
